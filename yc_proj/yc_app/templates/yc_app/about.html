State of The Yield Curve

After several episodes of the Planet Money Podcast discussed the Yield Curve, it inspired me to dig a bit deeper into the what it is and the data behind it. When I googled around and found only news articles, I was a bit frustrated by the lack of utility. I decided to create a site which pulls the daily yield curve data from the US Treasury Website and provides a deeper context to that data.

The site will grow over time, but I'm starting out by presenting a couple key views:

  1. A daily one-liner: "The Yield Curve is Inverted" and "The Yield Curve is Not Inverted" -- along with the daily yield curve data to support it.
  2. A daily "comparison grid" which displays each short term/long term comparison for the day. A negative comparison means that those values are "inverted."
  3. A historical trend for each term's yield, as well as each short term/long term comparison. So if you want to see how many times the 2yr:10yr comp has been inverted in the last 5 years, just click on that value in the grid!

If you would like to collaborate, contribute, provide feedback, or just think this thing is cool, please reach out to me on twitter @SoTYieldCurve!
